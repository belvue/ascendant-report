## Summary

Operators may adopt a **category-based funding summary** so contributors understand allocation without publishing full accounting. Below is an **example structure** - categories are **legitimate**; the recommendation is **transparency**, not a mandated split.

## Example template

| Category | Example description | Example public fields |
|----------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Infrastructure** | Cloud hosting, CDN, backups | $X - $Y/mo band; months covered |
| **Domains & services** | DNS, SaaS, monitoring | $X/mo or annual |
| **Hardware reserve** | Future replacement / upgrades | Target band; spend policy |
| **Development** | Code, content, tooling licenses | % of burn or $ band |
| **Stewardship** | GM support, moderation | Paid / unpaid policy |
| **Community operations** | Events, docs, prizes | Optional line |
| **Contingency reserve** | Long-term stability | Target months; draw rules |

**Illustrative priority statement (operator may edit):**

> “Monthly cloud and domain bills are funded first. Surplus inflows increase contingency reserve until the published target is met. Development and community lines are funded only after infrastructure and reserve targets are met, unless announced otherwise.”

## Implications

- Implements **PF-001**, **PF-003**, **PF-006** in one readable page.
- Amounts may be **bands** or **quarterly updates** - precision optional; **categories** are not.
