# DT-030: Donor notes may link Ko-fi identity to in-game characters

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Priority** | P3 - Informational |
| **Status** | Context (feed notes + reported chat - not identity verification) |
| **Component** | Ko-fi notes / community chat |
| **CWE** | N/A |

## Summary

Ko-fi display names are not verified account bindings. Some public support messages include **in-game character names in donation notes**; some payments may be on behalf of another player. Reported follow-up chat described recurring auto-support as **voluntary server-cost help** with **no in-game benefit claimed**. This supports a **narrow transparency point**: donations may be **socially linked** to player identities - not proof of **paid perks, RMT, or staff favoritism**.

## Example

**Observed patterns (reported, not re-litigated here):**

- Ko-fi donor display name + notes referencing character names
- In-game chat: donor describes payment as **voluntary server-cost help**, **no goods in exchange**
- Informal phrase suggesting donations are **not discussed publicly** - social sensitivity, **not misconduct proof**

**What this does NOT prove:**

- Donations exchanged for items, currency, or immunity
- Staff quid pro quo
- Criminal fraud

## Player scenario

**Context:** Community hears that “donors are known to staff.”

**What happens:**

1. Character-name Ko-fi notes (**DT-020**) reinforce linkage.
2. Without a **published no-perks policy**, players may speculate anyway.
3. Documented chat **weakens** quid-pro-quo theories but **does not replace** public policy.

**Not an exploit:** Context only.

## Implications

- **Operator:** Publish clear **no-benefit** policy (**DT-031**) and **name-field use** (**DT-020**).
- **Players:** Treat anecdotal chat as **non-authoritative** vs official Server Support text.

## Remediation

1. Align with **DT-020** / **DT-031** public statements.
2. Do **not** publish donor identities in operator response materials.

## Verification

- Server Support states whether staff **track** donors by character name internally and **for what purpose**.
