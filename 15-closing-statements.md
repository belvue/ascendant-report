# 15 - Closing statements

I had fun on this server. Good times, old friends and new. The same people who made that possible also made a lot of it a horrible experience once scrutiny landed.

I do not want Ascendant to fail. I want the operator and staff to do well by their players. That is why remediation came before public release, and why most of this package is a boring artifact trail instead of a call to burn it down.

EQEmu kept Brad McQuaid's world alive in a way the live game never could. The scene still needs a **portable, supportable login path** the community can audit and reuse. Pointing every patched client at a curated `.dev` directory with six servers, buried in Discord instead of the launcher, is not that path. The launcher and support page are more polished than the login story, hosting honesty, and enforcement record underneath. Confidence outran what the artifacts support. That is fixable. It was not fixed in private.

Consider this a **warning**, not an indictment. If you are having fun, keep playing. Do it with open eyes: your login path and accounts are more exposed than the marketing suggests, staff have promised you nothing in writing, and the record shows **asymmetric treatment** when players report problems versus when insiders stay on roster.


> "Just had to get your fill didn't you?" ;)
