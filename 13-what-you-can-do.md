# 13 - What you can do

## If you still play

1. **Unique password** for Ascendant / eqemulator.dev. Do not reuse eqemulator.net credentials.
2. **Read your `eqhost.txt`** after every patch. Compare to [03-login-trust-boundary](03-login-trust-boundary.md).
3. **Report bugs** in writing. Keep timestamps. Assume private reports may be reframed publicly.
4. **Ask staff on the record** in `#helpful-info` or forum: Where does login go? Where is the world hosted? What is Ko-fi money spent on?

## If you donate

1. Treat Ko-fi **public feed as not an audit log** (chapter 05).
2. Ask for a **cost breakdown**: OVH vs residential vs tools vs labor reserve.
3. Decide if MoA utility matches your ethics, regardless of forum "gift" language.

## If you share this report

1. Link chapters, not withheld screenshots.
2. Edit [01-why-this-exists](01-why-this-exists.md) and [01b-before-going-public](01b-before-going-public.md) for your voice if you fork the package.
3. Send factual corrections with artifacts. Update beats spin.

## If you operate a server (lessons)

- Publish login-path changes **before** CDN push.
- Separate hosting cost categories on the donate page.
- Do not expose admin or SSH on residential game IPs. Auth-gate control planes before you take donations.
- Do not mock players who report private bugs. You will get fewer private reports and more public dossiers like this one.

I would still prefer Ascendant fix these items and keep players. The investigators' first move was a private remediation list, not a public dossier. That path went moot when operator presence dropped, the home backend stayed vulnerable after ISP notice, and major releases kept shipping anyway. This package is public so players and donors get transparency anyway. If Straps publishes corrections, link them and update the record. Your move as a player, donor, or operator is whether that posture is good enough for you.

See [01b-before-going-public](01b-before-going-public.md) for the full framing.
